## 13. Quantitative anchors (REHDA surveys)

The KB is now anchored to two large-N homebuyer surveys and one industry-side survey:

**REHDA MAPEX April 2024 (n=563 at Pavilion Bukit Jalil)**
- 38% earn <RM5k/month, 38% aged 25-34
- 50% target RM300-600k, 59% installment budget RM1-2.5k
- 94% want Klang Valley; top Selangor areas: Petaling Jaya (25%), Shah Alam (24%), Puchong (8%)
- Top barrier: high property prices (52%), interest rates (30%), recession (26%)
- 76% want within 10km city centre, 46% want transit-oriented development

**REHDA MAPEX June 2024 (n=845 at Mid Valley)** — largest single source in KB
- 69% earn <RM9k/month, 59% aged 20-34, 60% Chinese ethnicity / 32% Malay
- 37% target RM300-500k, 43% installment budget RM1-2k
- 88% want KV; top 5: PJ (22%), KL (15%), Shah Alam (7%), Cheras (5%), Puchong (3%)
- Out-of-state demand: Negeri Sembilan 71% (Seremban 29%, Nilai 18%)
- Top barriers: high property prices (42%), interest rates (23%)
- 50% want walking-distance public transit (TOD), 74% want green features

**REHDA Property Industry Survey 1H 2025 (n=187 developers)** — supply side
- Take-up rate collapsed 55% → 24% (2H 2024 → 1H 2025)
- 12,938 units launched (down from 17,404)
- 57% of new launches priced RM300-700k; 1% above RM2.5m
- 52% of developers carry unsold completed inventory; 24% of unsold is >RM1m
- 74% of developers report rising costs; 51% squeezed by economic conditions
- Cost-cutting: recruitment freeze, retrenchment, project delays

The supply-demand mismatch is now numerically grounded: the affordability sweet spot (RM300-700k) accounts for both 57% of supply and ~50% of buyer target, but the take-up rate has collapsed to 24% — strong evidence that even properly-priced inventory is meeting hesitant buyers.

## 16. How to use this KB

- Each line in `kv_buyer_knowledge_base.jsonl` is a self-contained record with `__source_file` and (where relevant) `__provenance` for traceability.
- For Claude Code workflows, load the JSONL line-by-line and filter on `buyer_segment`, `klang_valley_areas_mentioned`, `government_scheme_signals`, or `__source_file == "group07_surveys.jsonl"` to pull cluster-specific or quantitative-only context.
- The cluster cheat-sheet (Section 12) maps to messaging strategy: C1 wants reassurance + transparency, C3 wants clear scheme eligibility, C4 wants entry-cost framing (monthly payment, sub-RM500k options), C2 wants honest data not hype.
- The REHDA surveys in `group07_surveys.jsonl` should be cited as the *quantitative authority* for any audience-level claim (e.g., "37% of KV homebuyers target RM300-500k properties" cites MAPEX June 2024 directly).
