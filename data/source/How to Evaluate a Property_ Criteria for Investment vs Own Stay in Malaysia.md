### How to Evaluate a Property: Criteria for Investment vs Own Stay in Malaysia

##### 1\. The Foundation: Intent and the 8+1 Property Filter

In the Malaysian real estate landscape, the most critical error a buyer can make is failing to define their "Intent" before signing a booking form. Intent is the non-negotiable first step because it dictates every subsequent financial and lifestyle decision. A property for "Own Stay" is driven by emotional validation and a space that fits your soul, whereas an "Investment" property is a cold, data-driven vehicle for cash flow. There is also the dangerous "Show Off" intent—buying a property to satisfy parents or social ego—which often leads to acquiring a financial liability rather than an asset.To navigate this, a systematic "8+1 Filter" serves as a rigorous screening process. Only when a property passes these benchmarks should you move from the "thinking" phase to the "execution" phase.

This filter acts as a strategic shield against emotional over-commitment, transitioning the buyer from a "dreamer" to a sophisticated investor who uses the 85% Rule.

##### 2\. The 85% Own Stay Rule: Balancing Lifestyle with Exit Strategy

The "85% Rule" is a safety net for homeowners. It acknowledges that while a "Dream Home" should have a budget "painful enough" to feel the pinch, it must also provide a pivot path. If life changes—relocation, marriage, or expansion—you need an exit strategy that doesn't involve "topping up" thousands of ringgit every month to keep the property.**The Formula:**   $$\\frac{\\text{Current Market Rent}}{\\text{Monthly Mortgage Installment}} \\geq 85\\%$$This threshold is critical because, in the first 10 years of a loan, approximately 80% of your monthly payment goes strictly toward interest. If the market rent covers 85% of your installment, the tenant is effectively paying off the interest portion of your debt. This allows for a "painless" upgrade path later.**Case Study: The "CY" Example**  A prospective buyer (CY) was considering a property with a RM 3,800 mortgage in Taman Desa. He had rented there for 6 years and knew the market rent was RM 3,000.

* **Calculation:**   $3,000 / 3,800 \= 78.9\\%$ .  
* **Strategy:**  Although slightly below the 85% benchmark, CY’s "informed decision level" was exceptionally high due to his tenure in the area. Given his stable career and high savings, the advice was: "Just buy the property, bro." The proximity to the benchmark, combined with local knowledge, mitigated the risk.

##### 3\. Quantitative Criteria for the High-Yield Investment Property

Investment selection is about the "temporary demand for space." You are looking for high-traffic demographics, not personal aesthetic satisfaction.

* **Rental Yield Benchmarks:**  Target  **5.4% to 6%** . With current interest rates, any yield lower than 5% creates negative cash flow.  
* **The University/Hospital & "Old Rich" Factor:**  Proximity to private universities (e.g., IMU) or hospitals (e.g., Columbia Asia) ensures a non-removable tenant pool. In areas like Bukit Jalil, demand is also driven by the "Old Rich" from nearby OUG/Sri Petaling buying units for their second-generation children to stay close to home.  
* **True TOD (Transit Oriented Development):**  A property is only a TOD if it is within  **400m**  of a station. Strategic examples like  **Lumio (Asia Jaya LRT)**  or  **Tropicana Gardens**  provide seamless, covered integration. Avoid "Fake TODs" that require crossing a highway under the hot sun.  
* **Investment Multipliers:**  Use bulk purchase routes to buy below market value. While high density (e.g., M-Vertica) is often criticized by laypeople, for an investor, it provides the massive facility decks and foot traffic that drive high rental demand.

##### 4\. Qualitative Criteria for the Ideal Own-Stay Property

An own-stay property is an expenditure for enjoyment. However, if the "dream" involves a 5-hour daily commute from the outskirts like  **Rawang or Kajang**  to a KL-based office, it becomes a "dreadful process" that destroys your quality of life.

* **The "Weekend vs. Weekday" Rule:**  Test the commute and amenities at peak and off-peak hours. If the traffic paralyzes your lifestyle, the property is a fail regardless of the price.  
* **Engineering over "Marketing Fluff":**  A sophisticated buyer looks at the  **refuse chamber design**  (ventilation) and  **car park lobby**  functionality. Developers often market "Avatar-themed gardens," but for long-term stay, functional infrastructure and low-density serenity (e.g., Desa ParkCity) are what actually preserve value.  
* **Maintenance Fees:**  Evaluate the hidden costs. Do not pay RM 0.40 psf for an Olympic pool you will never use. High-maintenance "gimmicks" eventually become a burden for the JMB and the homeowners.

##### 5\. Infrastructure Realities: The "Mall Curse" and TOD Traps

Proximity to amenities must be viewed through the lens of your intent:

* **The Mall Curse:**  For an  **Investor** , a mall is a blessing that ensures high rental demand and convenience. For an  **Own-Stay**  buyer, it is often a curse, bringing permanent traffic congestion, noise, and a lack of privacy.  
* **Access Points:**  Prioritize properties with at least  **three entry/exit points** . A "luxury" development with only one point of entry is prone to peak-hour paralysis, making the "luxury" lifestyle impossible to enjoy.

##### 6\. Red Flags: Schemes and Market Oversupply

Avoid "lazy investing" tactics that hide poor property fundamentals:

* **Guaranteed Rental Return (GRR):**  A classic trap where the "guaranteed" rent is simply priced into your purchase price. Once the period ends, you are left with a unit that cannot fetch market rent.  
* **Income Engineering:**  This "dodgy" practice involves faking EPF contributions or payslips to secure loans. It is loan fraud and a legal risk; if you cannot afford the property without engineering your income, the market will eventually punish you.  
* **Oversupply Segments:**  The  **RM 800k to RM 1.5M**  bracket is currently saturated. Additionally, be wary of certain areas with an oversupply of  **affordable housing (Rumah WIP)** , which can suppress the rental growth of nearby open-market condominiums.

##### 8\. Valuation Mastery: Avoiding the Overpriced Trap

Profit is made on the day you buy. If you pay a "fluffy" price for a new launch because of freebies, you've already lost your margin.

* **Data Accuracy:**  Use  **bricks.my**  to find the "Median Price" of a specific street or building. Never compare a 20-year-old building directly with a new launch; they exist in different market ecosystems.  
* **Budget 2026 Impacts:**  
* **Stamp Duty:**  The increase in Foreigner Stamp Duty from  **4% to 8%**  is a cooling measure, as the government anticipates a surge in demand from the MM2H program (Silver/Gold/Platinum tiers).  
* **Carbon Tax:**  The imposition of carbon taxes on iron and steel will inevitably increase  **construction costs** . This will push up the prices of new launches, which eventually pulls the subsale market upward.  
* **Mandatory Readiness Buffer:**  Never sign an SPA without a  **RM 50,000 renovation buffer**  and  **6 months of mortgage installments**  parked in a flexi-account. This ensures you can furnish the unit to a high standard—which is a business necessity to beat the competition—and handle any rental vacancies.**Summary:**  A sophisticated investor makes calculated decisions regardless of market noise. Whether it is a personal sanctuary or a cash-flow vehicle, ensure your intent is clear and your financial buffer is absolute.
