### Property Analysis Master Scoring Framework for Malaysia (2025-2026 Edition)

In the current Malaysian property landscape, valuation has transitioned from a simple comparison of price-per-square-foot (PSF) to a complex synthesis of demographic density and capital expenditure (CAPEX) modeling. As we navigate the 2025-2026 cycle—marked by the implementation of the Carbon Tax and revised stamp duty structures—investors must look beyond the "sticker price" to the intrinsic Net Present Value (NPV). Defining a buyer’s intent is the non-negotiable first step; it dictates whether we prioritize the "lifestyle certainty" of an "old rich" enclave like OUG or the "rental liquidity" of a Transit Oriented Development (TOD) hub like Bangsar South.

#### 1\. THE INTENT LAYER: Strategic Categorization

The weightage of any property metric shifts according to the acquisition goal. Without a locked intent, buyers fall into the "sister’s trap"—purchasing an outskirt landed property for "own stay" but suffering a 4-hour daily commute, only to find the unit has zero rental demand when they inevitably need to move closer to the city.| Profile | Definition | Strategic Weighting Priority || \------ | \------ | \------ || **Pure Investment** | Solely for yield/growth. Treats property as a business asset. | High: Rental liquidity, 500m TOD radius, Grade A office proximity. || **Pure Own Stay** | Personal residence for long-term (10+ years) settlement. | High: Low density, "Soul-fit," school catchments (e.g., Puchong Chinese schools). || **Hybrid** | Residence for 5–10 years with a mandated exit strategy. | Balanced: Must fulfill lifestyle needs while adhering to the "85% Rule." |  
**Weighting Logic & The "So What?"**  Investors must ignore emotional attachments to "serenity" and target high-friction "busy" hubs where the temporary demand for space is high. Conversely, own-stay buyers must find "lifestyle certainty"—the ability to customize a space to fit their soul, ensuring it serves as a productivity engine rather than a financial anchor. Once intent is locked, the financial threshold becomes the next non-negotiable barrier.

#### 2\. FINANCIAL READINESS SCORE: The "Financial Hygiene" Audit

*(Weighting: 25% of Final Score)*A lead strategist views pre-qualification not as a hurdle, but as a risk-mitigation audit. This prevents the "ego-trap" of booking a unit before calculating one's true borrowing capacity. High gross income is a vanity metric; disposable income and Credit Health (CCRIS/CTOS) are the only metrics that drive loan approval.

* **Debt Service Ratio (DSR):**  Total monthly commitments must remain below 70% of net income.  
* **Liquidity Buffer (The 6+6 Rule):**  6 months of total living expenses \+ 6 months of mortgage installments. This provides a hedge against interest rate fluctuations or vacancy periods.  
* **Net Present Value (NPV) of Entry Capital:**  Minimum 10% down payment \+ 10% for "hidden costs" (MOT, legal fees, valuation, and stamp duty).  
* **The BNPL Constraint:**  "Buy Now, Pay Later" schemes and small monthly installments (electronics/furniture) are flagged by bankers and can drop your credit score (Band 1–10), leading to lower interest rate entitlements.*Transition: Financial hygiene ensures you can capitalize on price realism rather than chasing inflated "rebate" schemes.*

#### 3\. PROPERTY PRICE SCORE: Market Value Realism

*(Weighting: 15% of Final Score)*In the 2026 horizon, construction costs are projected to trend upward due to the  **Carbon Tax**  on iron, steel, and energy sectors. This "green premium" will be passed from producers to developers, and ultimately to the end-buyer.

* **Brickz Data Comparison:**  Purchase price must be within 10–20% of the median transacted price for the postcode. A new launch at RM900 PSF in an area where the median is RM450 PSF (e.g., specific segments of Bukit Jalil) creates extreme negative equity risk.  
* **The "Rebate" Critique:**  Analyze if "zero down payment" offers are genuine value or merely priced-in premiums to cover developer marketing and 8% non-resident stamp duty costs.  
* **PSF Delta Analysis:**  Compare target PSF against 10-year-old comparable projects. If the gap exceeds 50%, the new project must justify it with superior facade specifications or tech-integration.  
* **Red Flag:**   **Guaranteed Rental Return (GRR)**  is an automatic fail. It suggests the property cannot survive on actual market demand.

#### 4\. THE LOCATION SCORE: Connectivity & Amenity Density

*(Weighting: 30% of Final Score)*Property value growth is driven by the "Mountain and River" theory: Circulation/Access (Rivers) versus Population Density (Mountains).

1. **TOD & Public Transport:**  500m (Premium) vs. 1km (Acceptable) to MRT/LRT/BRT.  
2. **Highway Connectivity:**  Multiple entry/exit points; avoid "single road" developments (permanent trap zones).  
3. **Puchong Infrastructure Nuance:**  Note that Puchong has  **6 LRT stations** , making it a connectivity outlier compared to addresses like Cheras.  
4. **Anchor Amenities:**  2 hospitals, malls, or schools within 1km.  
5. **Mall Vitality Audit:**  Check the  **Retail Refreshment Rate**  (how fast new tenants replace old ones). A "dying" mall is a leading indicator of neighborhood decay.  
6. **Employment Hubs:**  Proximity (5km) to Grade A offices.  
7. **Environmental/Soil Audit:**  Do not rely on social media panic regarding sinkholes. Audit soil stability via town planning maps to check for  **mining land history** .  
8. **Future Infrastructure:**  Cross-reference with MRT3, LRT3, or RTS (Johor Bahru) masterplans.  
9. **Land Scarcity:**  Identify if surrounding land is Greenbelt (value protector) or Open (oversupply risk).  
10. **Social Demographics:**  "Old Rich" addresses (OUG, Bangsar) vs. pet-friendly enclaves (Desa Park City) create distinct valuation premiums.

#### 5\. PROPERTY PRODUCT SCORE: Physical Asset Evaluation

*(Weighting: 15% of Final Score)*"Architecture Matters" because layout efficiency dictates own-stay comfort and long-term resale liquidity.

* **Layout Efficiency:**  Measure the "Width vs. Depth" ratio. A  **4.3m living room width**  is the institutional benchmark for "atas" (premium) feel vs. the 2.9m standard.  
* **Facade Area (CAPEX Proxy):**  High glass-to-concrete ratios on the facade indicate a developer "flexing" their capital expenditure, which correlates with higher long-term value.  
* **Developer Audit:**  Distinguish between "Institutional" and "Hobbyist" developers. Institutional players do not require  **bridging loans** , meaning they won't panic-sell or slash prices, effectively protecting your asset’s price floor.  
* **Car Parks:**  Side-by-side parking carries a premium; tandem parking is a liquidity inhibitor.  
* **Maintenance:**  Benchmark  **RM0.40 PSF**  as the threshold for building health.

#### 6\. INVESTMENT-SPECIFIC SCORE: The "85% Rule"

*(Weighting: 15% for Investment Intent)*Investment is a business; emotional bias must be replaced with cash flow modeling.

* **The 85% Rule:**  Neighborhood rental must cover 85% of the mortgage installment. Note: In the first 10 years,  **interest can comprise \~88% of the installment** . The 85% rule acts as an NPV hedge against holding costs.  
* **Yield Benchmarks & Action Tiers:**| Yield Tier | Rating | Investor Action || \------ | \------ | \------ || $\\geq$  6% | **Strong** | Accumulate; prime for compounding. || $\\geq$  5% | **Good** | Solid hold; benchmark for stable urban areas. || $\\geq$  4% | **Acceptable** | Standard; evaluate for capital growth potential. || $\<$  4% | **Weak** | Exit or pivot strategy required. |  
* **Pivot Options:**  Can the unit switch between student rental (near IMU/Sunway), co-living, or Airbnb?  
* **Oversupply Audit:**  Verify NAPIC data for overhang risk (\>30% vacancy in postcode).

#### 7\. OWN STAY-SPECIFIC SCORE: Lifestyle & Emotional Fit

For the homeowner, property provides "Lifestyle Certainty"—control over your environment that rental cannot provide.

* **The 30-Minute Rule:**  Workplace commute must be under 30 minutes.  
* **School Proximity:**  Primary/Secondary schools within 3km.  
* **Environmental Noise Audit:**  Site visits during peak weekday and weekend hours to assess highway noise or industrial odors.  
* **Exit Hedge:**  Even for own-stay, the 85% rule must apply. This ensures that if you move (e.g., for a job in Singapore), the property can self-sustain as a rental.

#### 8\. RED FLAGS CHECKLIST: The Auto-Fail Conditions

These conditions supersede all positive scores. If any are met, the verdict is an automatic  **AVOID** .

* **Guaranteed Rental Return (GRR) Schemes.**  
* **Single Entry/Exit Road**  (Permanent traffic bottleneck).  
* **Price \> 20% Above Neighborhood Median**  (Negative equity/refinance risk).  
* **Abandoned Project History**  or developer instability.  
* **Leasehold \< 60 Years**  (Future financing/resale difficulty).  
* **Oversupply**  (\>30% vacancy in the specific postcode).

#### 9\. FINAL SCORING OUTPUT FORMAT

For any specific property analysis, the final output must be presented as follows:

1. **Overall Score:**  X/100  
2. **Intent Match:**  Pure Investment / Pure Own Stay / Hybrid  
3. **Top 3 Reasons TO Buy:**  (e.g., High facade CAPEX, 85% Rule compliant, Institutional developer).  
4. **Top 3 Risks / Concerns:**  (e.g., Carbon tax price inflation, mining land history, high maintenance PSF).  
5. **Verdict:**  Strong Buy / Consider / Needs More Research / Avoid*Note: The Verdict is derived from weighted scores: Location (30%), Financial (25%), Price (15%), Product (15%), and Intent-Specific (15%).*
